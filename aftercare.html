Tattoo Aftercare

Now that your tattoo is complete, it’s important to follow aftercare closely. Your fresh tattoo is an open wound and needs proper care to avoid infection and complications. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Adhesive Film Wet Heal Dry Heal

If you think your tattoo is infected, seek medical assistance ASAP. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Adhesive Film

Initial Application

  1. Cut/trim film to fit around the tattoo (add ~1 inch on all sides). Round the corners for better comfort/adhesion. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  2. Peel the white paper from the sticky side first and place gently over the tattoo. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  3. Remove the top layer and smooth the bandage over the tattoo. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  4. Keep the first bandage on 3–24 hours depending on weeping; replace within 24 hours. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Removal + Reapply 2nd Bandage

  1. Remove by pulling back over itself (direction of hair growth). Shower removal is ideal. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  2. Wash with mild, preferably fragrance-free soap. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  3. Air dry or pat dry with a clean towel. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  4. Optional: thin layer of aftercare product; avoid getting it on surrounding skin if reapplying bandage. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  5. Apply second bandage. Keep it on 3–6 days (6 ideal). Remove sooner if irritation/fluid buildup/peeling edges. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

After final removal

Continue washing/moisturizing as needed until fully healed. Surface may look healed sooner than deeper layers. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Showering / Swimming / Exercise

  • Showering is fine, but keep showers short; water/heat can weaken adhesive. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Avoid submerging (baths, swimming, etc.) while wearing film. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
  • Light exercise is OK; avoid heavy sweating. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Wet Heal

Follow these guidelines closely. A beautiful tattoo can turn into a disaster if aftercare isn’t followed. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

  • Leave the initial bandage on 3–5 hours to protect from airborne bacteria. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
  • Remove bandage, wash with lukewarm water + antibacterial soap (use your hand; no abrasive cloth). :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}
  • Pat dry (don’t rub). Air 10–15 minutes, apply a very thin layer of aftercare balm, and rewrap with quality film. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}
  • Repeat every few hours for 3–4 days. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}
  • After wet healing: wash, pat dry, light balm; continue 2–3x/day for 5–7 days or until healed. :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
  • Days 5–10: apply balm/cream at least 2x/day. Days 11–21: clean + moisturize at least daily. :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}
  • Avoid long showers/baths for 2 weeks; avoid swimming/ocean/pool/spas/saunas. Keep showers short (under ~10 min). :contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}
  • Peeling/scabbing can be normal. Don’t pick or scratch. Moisturize if peeling; leave scabs alone. :contentReference[oaicite:23]{index=23}
  • After healed: protect from UV; use 30+ SPF when in heavy sun/heat. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24}

Dry Heal

This isn’t the preferred method, but may be needed for allergies/sensitivities. If possible, wet heal or use transparent wrap. :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}

  • After removing bandage: wash with lukewarm water + antibacterial soap (no abrasive cloth). :contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26}
  • Pat dry (don’t rub), apply a very light layer of tattoo balm/cream. :contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27}
  • Continue at least 3x/day for 5–7 days or until healed. :contentReference[oaicite:28]{index=28}
  • Days 5–10: balm/cream at least 2x/day. Days 11–21: clean + moisturize daily. :contentReference[oaicite:29]{index=29}
  • Avoid long showers/baths for 2 weeks; avoid soaking/swimming/spas/saunas. Short showers are best. :contentReference[oaicite:30]{index=30}
  • Peeling/scabbing can happen; don’t pick/scratch. Moisturize peeling skin; leave scabs alone. :contentReference[oaicite:31]{index=31}
